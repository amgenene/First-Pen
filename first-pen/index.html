TimeLine of Neil's accomplishments

  • In 1994 Tyson focused his Princeton research cosmology stellar evolution, and galactic astronomy.
  • In 1995 he was the acting director of the planetarium and was promoted to director the next year
  • In 1996 as director of the planetarium Neil was in charge of a 210 million reconstruction project, which was completed in 2000.
  • From 1995-2000 Neil was also writing in columns of the Natural history magazine, specifically the "Universe" columns
  • In 2001 George W. Bush appointed Neil to serve in the commision on the future of the United States Aerospace Industry
  • In 2002 he coined the term "Manhattanhedge", which described the two days that the sun aligned with the street grid, which allowed the sunset to be visible in obstructed side streets.
  • 2004 was a very eventful year for Neil Degrasse Tyson as he served on the President's Commision on Implemention of United States Space Exploration Policy. At the same Neil was also hosting the four part PBS television series called Nova.
  • Neil collaberated with Donald Goldsmith to bring us the documentary 400 Years of the Telescope which premeired on PBS April of 2009.
  • In his time at the Heydan Planetarium Neil went against popular belief and dropped Pluto from the planets list. His reasoning for this was after careful inspections of the characteristics that grouped terrestrial planets together and Pluto did not fit any of the categories.(He was later affirmed by the Internation Astronomical Union, by changing Pluto to a dwarf planet classification.)
  • In 2006 Neil was the speaker at the Beyond Belief Science, Religion, Reason and Survival symposium
  • In 2007 he was a regular on the History channel's "The Universe"
  • In 2009 he launched a radio talk show called StarTalk with Lynne Koplitz, which lasted for 13 weeks and became syndicated on Sundays afternoons in on KTLK AM in Los Angeles and WHFS in Washington DC.
  • In 2011 Neil was the speaker at the 93rd International Convention of the Phi Theta Kappa International Honor Society of the Two-year School
  • In 2014 he was a guest at White House Student Film Festival, as well as reviving Carl Sagan's Cosmos A Personal Voyage television series, n FOX and the National Geographic Channel.
  • On April 2015 he began hosting a late night talk show called Star Talk on National Geographic channel, where Tyson interviews celebrities and talks about their experiences with science.
